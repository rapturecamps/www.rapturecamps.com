# Rapture Surfcamps vs Dreamsea Surf Camp — Comprehensive Comparison

## TL;DR

Rapture is a veteran operator (est. 2003) with 20+ years of experience, focused on premium surf destinations in Bali, Portugal, and Costa Rica with a strong emphasis on surf coaching quality, included meals, and an intimate community feel. Dreamsea (est. 2013) is a younger, Europe-heavy brand with 14 locations across 8 countries, known for glamping/outdoor accommodation and a sustainability-first, family-friendly positioning. Rapture gives you deeper surf immersion with more included in the package; Dreamsea gives you more destination choices and a distinctive outdoor/nature-focused experience.

## Detailed Comparison

### 1. Destinations & Reach

**Rapture** operates a curated network focused on quality over quantity. Their primary hubs are:
- **Bali** — Green Bowl (Bukit, beachfront) + Padang Padang (boutique villa, barrel-focused)
- **Portugal** — Ericeira (World Surfing Reserve, 6 room types), Coxos Surf Villa (premium), Milfontes (Alentejo, eco/nature)
- **Costa Rica** — Avellanas (Guanacaste, jungle setting, outdoor cinema, recovery centre)
- Additional presence in Morocco and Nicaragua

**Dreamsea** has the larger footprint with 14 locations:
- **Europe-heavy**: 3x Portugal, 4x Spain (Cantabria, Fuerteventura, Gran Canaria, Mediterranean), 1x France, 1x Morocco
- **Americas**: 2x Costa Rica (Avellanas + Santa Teresa), 1x Nicaragua
- **Asia**: 1x Bali (Uluwatu), 1x Sri Lanka

**Verdict**: Dreamsea wins on sheer destination variety, especially for European travelers wanting to stay close to home. Rapture wins on depth of the surf experience at each location and offers more variety within key regions (e.g., multiple distinct camps in Bali's best surf zone).

---

### 2. Accommodation

**Rapture** offers:
- Shared rooms (4–6 beds), twin/double, private rooms, glamping tents
- Linen, towels, daily cleaning included
- AC on indoor rooms; fans for glamping
- Range from social dorms to premium villas (Coxos Surf Villa with infinity pool)

**Dreamsea** is the glamping specialist:
- **Glamping tents** — canvas/natural fiber with cozy decor, mattresses, bedding, towels, welcome pack (bottle of wine!)
- **Shared tents** — budget-friendly, 1–6 people, heart of the camp
- **Surf houses** — Costa Rica + Sintra; proper houses in strategic surf locations
- **Surf hotels & lodges** — Bali (beachfront cliff hotel), Sri Lanka, Nicaragua (bamboo lodge), Gran Canaria
- **Bungalows** — Morocco + Portugal Alentejo

**Verdict**: Dreamsea has more varied and distinctive accommodation types, with glamping as a genuine USP. Rapture offers a more traditional range but with higher-end premium options (Coxos villa, Green Bowl beachfront).

---

### 3. Surf Coaching

**Rapture**:
- Lessons grouped by skill level
- ~Two sessions per day (morning 2–3h, afternoon 1.5–2h)
- Max 5:1 student-to-instructor ratio
- All equipment included (boards, wetsuits, rash guards, booties)
- Surf theory + weekly video analysis included in base package
- Surf guiding available for an extra fee
- Live 14-day surf forecast on website

**Dreamsea**:
- 3-tier level system (Level 0/1/2) designed by Physical Education professionals
- Group lessons with 1:4 ratio (Bali) to 1:8 (Portugal camps)
- Courses sold separately from accommodation: Short (2–3 lessons), Basic (5 lessons), Intensive (7 lessons)
- Private lessons available (€40/2h in Bali)
- Video analysis only at Level 2
- Free board rental (softboards + Pristine retro boards) included in weekly packages
- Surfskate sessions included in Level 1/2 at European camps

**Verdict**: Rapture offers a more comprehensive surf package with coaching included in the base price, better student-to-instructor ratios, and video analysis for everyone. Dreamsea separates accommodation from surf courses, which adds flexibility but means the total cost for a full surf experience is higher. Dreamsea's unique surfskate integration is a nice differentiator.

---

### 4. Food & Dining

**Rapture**:
- Breakfast + dinner Mon–Fri included in standard packages
- 3-course breakfasts with full spread (fruit, eggs, smoothie bowls, unlimited coffee)
- 3-course dinners with rotating menus
- Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free options available
- Farm-to-table angle at Milfontes

**Dreamsea**:
- Varies dramatically by location:
  - **Bali**: Breakfast only included in accommodation price; restaurant/tiki bar open 7am–10pm with Indonesian + international dishes
  - **European camps (Portugal Centro)**: Full board included (7 breakfasts, 7 lunch boxes, 6 dinners)
  - Food described as "home-made, fresh, seasonal, balanced, healthy"
  - Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free (on request) options

**Verdict**: In Europe, Dreamsea actually includes more (lunch boxes!) while Rapture includes dinner. In Bali, Rapture is much more inclusive with breakfast + dinner vs. Dreamsea's breakfast-only model. Overall Rapture's consistent meal inclusion across destinations is simpler to understand and better value.

### 5. Pricing

**Bali comparison (1 week, shared room):**
| | Rapture | Dreamsea |
|---|---|---|
| Accommodation + meals + surf | ~€500 all-in | €484 (weekly pack) or €245 (acc + breakfast only) + €200 surf course = €445–684 |
| What's included | Accommodation, breakfast + dinner, surf lessons/guiding, yoga, video analysis | Accommodation, breakfast + 5 dinners, free surf equipment, no surf course |

**Portugal comparison (1 week, shared/glamping):**
| | Rapture | Dreamsea |
|---|---|---|
| Shared room/tent | ~€500 all-in | €322–462 (shared tent, full board, no surf) |
| Add surf course | Included | +€170–280 depending on level/duration |

**Verdict**: Rapture's pricing is simpler (one all-in rate). Dreamsea's modular pricing can be cheaper if you don't want surf lessons, but adds up quickly when you include courses. For a full surf camp experience, prices end up comparable.

---

### 6. Community & Vibe

**Rapture**: Positioned as a surf-first community for active travelers. ~60% solo travelers at Costa Rica. Digital nomad friendly. 80k+ past guests. Strong review presence (23 TripAdvisor awards). Retreat hosting available for groups.

**Dreamsea**: Family-founded, warmth-centered brand. Strong "family" messaging. Sustainability and nature at the core. Live bands, BBQs, karaoke, movie nights, skateboarding, volleyball. More festival/outdoor-camp vibe in Europe; boutique hotel vibe in Asia. Active Instagram community. Surfskate culture integration.

**Verdict**: Rapture skews more toward serious surf travelers and digital nomads. Dreamsea skews toward nature lovers, families, and festival-atmosphere seekers. Both welcome solo travelers.

---

### 7. Sustainability

**Rapture**: Not heavily marketed on the website, though eco angle present at Milfontes (eco rooms, farm setting, nature park).

**Dreamsea**: Sustainability is a core brand pillar:
- Vernacular bamboo/natural architecture
- Dismantable glamping structures (no environmental trace)
- Partnerships with local NGOs
- Beach cleanings, education programs
- Own sustainable surf brand collaborations (Deeply wetsuits)
- Prominent sustainability page and messaging throughout

**Verdict**: Dreamsea wins here. Sustainability is deeply integrated into their brand identity and operations.

## Who Should Choose Rapture Over Dreamsea?

- **Dedicated surfers** who want coaching included and better instructor ratios
- **Travelers seeking all-inclusive simplicity** (one price, everything included)
- **Those wanting premium Bali surf access** (Bukit/Padang Padang vs. Uluwatu only)
- **People who prefer traditional accommodation** (rooms, villas) over tents/glamping
- **Repeat surf travelers** who value 20+ years of operational experience
- **German-speaking travelers** (full DE website + content)
- **Groups wanting to host retreats** (dedicated retreat hosting program)

## Who Should Choose Dreamsea Over Rapture?

- **Budget-conscious travelers** who just want accommodation without a full surf package
- **European travelers** wanting nearby destinations (France, Spain mainland, Canaries)
- **Glamping/nature enthusiasts** who want a unique outdoor accommodation experience
- **Families** — Dreamsea is more explicitly family-friendly at most European locations
- **Sustainability-focused travelers** who prioritize eco-credentials
- **Those wanting destination variety** — 14 locations vs. 6–8
- **Mediterranean/non-surf travelers** — Dreamsea has non-surf options (water sports at Mediterranean camp)
